Beno HRMS — how the system works

This document explains the full system in plain language — what it is, how it's built, who uses it, and what order everything happens in. Written for CEOs, PMs, and anyone who wants to understand without reading code.

📅 May 2026
👥 For: CEO, PM, Tech Lead
🌍 Scope: Global enterprise SaaS
What is this product?
Think of Beno like a building management company. We build and rent out office buildings (the HRMS software). A big group like Tata comes to us, signs a contract, and we give them access. Inside that building, Tata can set up multiple floors — one floor for Tata Steel, one for Tata Tea, one for Tata Motors. Each floor (company) runs its own HR — hiring people, tracking attendance, processing salaries. We manage the building. They manage their people.

e.g. Tata Tea admin. Manages their one company. Sets up LOB, Unit, Dept, roles, config.

Approves leave requests from their team (L1 approval). Views team attendance.
★ Employee

Daily operations — what happens every day
The employee has joined. Now the system runs daily. Three things happen: attendance is tracked, leaves are managed, and at end of month payroll is processed. These three are connected — leave affects attendance, attendance affects payroll.
Exists in code
Attendance
Employee checks in and out daily. System records: present, absent, late, WFH, overtime. Public holidays are auto-marked. Manager can correct mistakes.
Like a punch card machine at a factory. Every day it records if you showed up, when, and for how long.
Models: Attendance, AttendancePolicy, HolidayCalendar
Exists in code
Leave
Employee applies for leave. Manager approves (L1). HR approves (L2). Balance is deducted automatically. Types: annual, sick, casual, maternity, etc. Each has its own rules.
Like booking a holiday from work. You request it, your boss says yes, then HR confirms. Your remaining days go down automatically.
Models: LeaveType, LeavePolicy, LeaveRequest, LeaveBalance
Partial — payslip missing

At end of month: counts working days, deducts LOP for absences, applies PF / ESI / TDS, calculates net salary, generates payslip for each employee.
Like a shopkeeper counting the till at end of day — but for salaries. Count what was earned, subtract deductions, pay the rest.

The one rule that holds the whole system together
Every single piece of data belongs to one company. An employee belongs to Tata Tea — not Tata Steel. A leave request belongs to Tata Tea. A payslip belongs to Tata Tea. In the database, every record has a tenantId field — this is like a stamp on every document that says which company it belongs to. This means Tata Steel can never accidentally see Tata Tea's data. It's the foundation of the entire multi-tenant architecture. Remove this stamp from any record and the whole system breaks.
